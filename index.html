Advanced Microchannel Heat Exchangers

2021 International Journal of Heat and Mass Transfer

Abstract

This paper presents a novel design for microchannel heat exchangers that improves thermal performance by 35% while reducing pressure drop by 20% compared to conventional designs. The enhanced performance is achieved through a combination of optimized channel geometry, surface modifications, and innovative flow distribution techniques.

Key Innovations

  • Developed a hierarchical microchannel structure that enhances heat transfer while minimizing pressure drop
  • Implemented surface microstructures that promote nucleation and improve boiling heat transfer
  • Designed a novel flow distribution manifold that ensures uniform flow across all channels
  • Utilized additive manufacturing techniques to create complex internal geometries not possible with traditional manufacturing

Results

Experimental testing demonstrated a 35% improvement in overall heat transfer coefficient compared to conventional microchannel designs. Pressure drop was reduced by 20% due to the optimized flow distribution. The heat exchanger maintained stable performance over 1000 thermal cycles with no degradation.

Applications

This technology has applications in electronics cooling, automotive thermal management, aerospace systems, and HVAC applications where compact, high-performance heat exchangers are required.

Hybrid Solar-Thermal Energy Storage System

2022 Applied Energy

Abstract

This research presents a hybrid solar-thermal energy storage system that combines phase change materials with sensible heat storage to achieve 80% thermal efficiency and 24-hour heat supply capability for residential applications. The system addresses the intermittency of solar energy by providing consistent thermal output.

System Design

  • Integrated evacuated tube solar collectors with high-efficiency absorption
  • Developed a cascaded phase change material (PCM) system with multiple melting points
  • Incorporated a sensible heat storage tank as a buffer and for low-grade heat storage
  • Implemented an intelligent control system that optimizes energy flow based on demand and solar input

Performance

Field testing demonstrated an overall system efficiency of 80%, with the ability to provide 24-hour heat supply even during periods of limited solar input. The cascaded PCM system improved energy density by 40% compared to single-PCM systems, while the intelligent control reduced auxiliary energy consumption by 30%.

Impact

This system has the potential to reduce residential heating energy consumption by 60-70% in moderate climates. The technology is particularly suitable for regions with high solar insolation but limited grid infrastructure, providing a sustainable alternative to fossil fuel-based heating.
